Derawan Island

About Derawan Island

Derawan Island, located off the coast of East Kalimantan, Indonesia, is a secluded paradise known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and incredible marine life. As part of the Derawan Islands archipelago, Derawan Island is particularly renowned for its calm and beautiful surroundings, offering a tranquil escape for nature lovers and beach enthusiasts. The island is also a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its coral reefs, sea turtles, and vibrant underwater ecosystems.

Top Attractions

  • Pristine Beaches - The island’s beaches are untouched and perfect for relaxation and beach activities.
  • Snorkeling and Diving - Derawan is a world-class destination for underwater exploration, with its colorful coral reefs and abundant marine life.
  • Sea Turtle Watching - Derawan Island is one of the best places to see sea turtles up close, especially during nesting season.
  • Marine Conservation - The island is home to important conservation efforts aimed at preserving the diverse marine ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Derawan Island is during the dry season, which lasts from April to October. The weather is ideal for outdoor activities, and the sea conditions are perfect for diving and snorkeling. The rainy season from November to March sees fewer tourists, but heavy rainfall can disrupt activities, making it a less favorable time for travelers.

Local Culture and Cuisine

The local culture of Derawan Island is influenced by indigenous communities who primarily engage in fishing and small-scale farming. The island's cuisine consists of fresh seafood, including grilled fish, squid, and lobster, as well as traditional Indonesian dishes such as nasi goreng (fried rice) and sate (grilled skewered meat). Be sure to try the local delicacy of freshly prepared ikan bakar (grilled fish).

Travel Tips

- Respect local customs and the environment by avoiding damage to coral reefs and marine life.
- The island is remote, so it’s recommended to bring cash as ATMs may not be available.
- Bring appropriate swimwear, sunscreen, and protective gear for outdoor activities like snorkeling and diving.
